Inflation Rate Dashboard

Inflation is an measurement of rate of increasing price of goods and services. For example, the daily need-based expenses such as food, utilities and housing. If inflation occurs, the prices of basic necessities will increase and huge negative impacts will be resulted. There are some factors that will causes inflation such as demand and production costs. Besides the factors that affected inflation, inflation can be measured by using the Consumer Price Index (CPI). Lastly, there are four visualizations that showing about inflation rate in Australia and global. (Investopedia, 2022)

Inflation Rate of Countries in year 2021

The graph below shows the inflation rate of countries in the year 2021. Next, there are 179 countries in the map chart below. However, there are four countries that have text annotations. For example, Australia, Brazil, Canada and China. Besides that, the inflation rate of each country is differentiated by colour saturation. The lower the colour saturation, the lower the value of the inflation rate of the following country.

Quarterly Percentage Change by capital city of Australia

The graph below shows the Quarterly Percentage Change by capital city of Australia. Next, there are four types of groups in the scatter graph. Based on the scatter graph below, it shows that the alcohol and tobacco group has the lowest value of quaterly percentage change compared with other groups. Lastly, there is a drop down menu that can be selected by user for the specific group.

CPI Annual Movement of Goods and Services in Australia

The graph below shows the Consumer Price Index (CPI) annual movement of goods and services in Australia. Next, the values of the CPI Annual Movement of Goods and Services are showing from the year 2003 to the year 2021. Based on the stacked bar chart below, it proves that the value of CPI Annual Movement of Goods and Services in the year 2008 is the highest which is 4.4 in total. Conversely, the value of CPI Annual Movement of Goods and Services in the year 2020 is the lowest which is 0.8 in total. Lastly, either the values of the CPI Annual Movement of Goods or the values of the CPI Annual Movement of Services can be shown by selecting the Category legend.

CPI Percentage Change (%) for year 2022 in Australia

The graph below shows the Consumer Price Index (CPI) Percentage Change (%) for the year 2022 in Australia. Next, four months are shown in the heatmap graph below which are March, June, September and December. Based on the heatmap graph below, it shows that the value of the Consumer Price Index (CPI) Percentage Change (%) on 8th September 2022 is the highest which is 5%. Conversely, the value of the Consumer Price Index (CPI) Percentage Change (%) on 20th June 2022 is the lowest which is -0.3%. Lastly, the heatmap graph of a specific month can be shown by selecting the Month legend.
